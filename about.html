Why I Do This Work

I started building AI systems for myself. Not because I was trying to start a business—because I was overwhelmed.

As a working mom running multiple projects, I was drowning in information. Meeting notes scattered across apps. Important emails buried in my inbox. Knowledge trapped in my head that I couldn't easily share with collaborators.

So I built something. A daily briefing agent that reads my emails, checks my calendar, pulls from my task lists, and sends me a "Good Morning, Mom" summary every day at 6am. It tells me what's urgent, what I'm forgetting, and what my kids have going on.

Then I built more. Agents that draft in my voice. Systems that organize my knowledge so I can actually find things. Workflows that do the repetitive work so I can focus on what matters.

And I realized: if this transformed my own work, it could transform others' too.

My Approach

I believe AI should amplify human potential, not replace human judgment.

That means I don't sell "AI transformation" in the abstract. I build real systems that solve real problems—and I make sure you can maintain them without me.

My work follows a simple principle: Build with you, then hand off.

You're not hiring a consultant who creates dependency. You're hiring a partner who transfers capability. When we're done, you own the system, understand how it works, and can evolve it as your business grows.
